CHEM 370 Lab Manual

Materials

  • 2 M HCl
  • Stock: 100 ppm Phosphorous in distilled water from KH2PO4
  • 2.5 M sulfuric acid
  • Prepare a solution of 25 mM ammonium heptamolybdate

Preparation of standards for the calibration curve: Standard phosphate solutions are prepared from a 100 ppm stock solution made by dissolving 0.11 g potassium dihydrogen orthophosphate in 250 mL deion. H2O (Stock solution A). Accurately transfer 10 mL from stock solution A into a 250 mL volumetric flask and dilute it up to the mark with deion. H2O (Stock solution B). Use stock solution B to prepare standard solutions by pipetting 5, 10, 15, 20 respectively 25 mL portions into separate 100 mL volumetric flasks. Preparation of sample: Dilute the sample 1:100 with deion. H2O. Pipette 25 mL of the diluted sample in a 100 mL volumetric flask

Combined Reagent: combine 50 mL of 2.5 M H2SO4, 5 mL potassium antimony tartrate solution (i.e. dissolve 0.1371 g C8H4K2O12Sb2 x H2O in 50 mL) and 15 mL ammonium molybdate solution (2 g (NH4)6Mo7O6.4H2O in 50 mL of deion. H2O), 25 mL ascorbic acid solution (i.e. dissolve 0.528 g of ascorbic acid in 25 mL of deion. H2O) and 5 ml deion. H2O. The solution is thoroughly shaken.

Color development:

  1. Prepare a 1000-ppm P\(_2\)O\(_5\) stock solution from KH\(_2\)PO\(_4\) using deionized water and a volumetric flask. (1.9412 g of dry KH2PO4, reagent grade, and dissolve in deionized water in a 1-L volumetric flask)
