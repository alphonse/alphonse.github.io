CHEM 370 Lab Manual

4.1 HPLC

This lab will use the Agilent 1220 HPLC (high performance liquid chromatograph) to determine the concentration of acetaminophen in Tylenol tablets.

Review how to perform serial dilutions for a standard curve.

Safety

This lab will require the use water and methanol for solvents. Methanol can cause blindness and absorbs through the skin. Wear gloves and goggles when working with methanol, and use it in a hood.

4.1.1 Standard Preparation

  1. Make 100 ml of 100-ppm (w/v) acetaminophen stock solution using 98% 4-acetamidophenol in ultra pure water.
  2. Perform serial dilutions to create a set of 5 standards with concentrations ranging from 0.5 - 25 ppm. You will need 2 ml of each standard for HPLC analysis, but it won’t hurt to have a little extra.

4.1.2 Sample Preparation

  1. Determine the mass of all the contents of your vials.
  2. Grind your sample into a fine, homogenous powder using a mortar and pestle.
  3. Cone and quarter your sample (on weighing paper) until you have just over 100 mg.
  4. Dissolve 100.0 mg of the tablet in 1:3 methanol:water, and bring the final volume to 100.0 ml.
  5. Perform a 1:100 dilution of you sample in 1:3 methanol:water.
  6. Filter final dilution through a 0.45 \(\mu\)m syringe filter using a 20 ml syringe. Make sure you:
    • Don’t pull up on the plunger with the filter attached to the syringe (you may tear the filter).
    • Discard the first 10 ml of filtrate.
    • Use an aliquot of the last 10 ml of filtrate for analysis.

4.1.3 Instrumentation

  1. Place approximately 2 ml of your final dilution in an autosampler vial; you should prepare at least 3 vials to run in triplicate.
  2. Prepare the appropriate blanks, using 1:3 methanol:water as your blank solution. Please be sure to filter your blanks before analysis.
  3. Run your samples according to the HPLC SOP, making sure you run:
    1. a rinse blank,
    2. a subtraction blank,
    3. your standards (from low to high),
    4. a rinse blank,
    5. your samples, and
    6. a final rinse blank.

4.1.4 Waste Disposal and Cleanup

  • Collect all wastes in the bottle labelled “Hazardous Waste: Lab 2 Acetaminophen” that is stored under the sample prep hood in the Instrument Lab.
  • Empty sample vials into the same waste container and dispose of glass in the broken glass bin.
  • Ensure your work area and the instrument are cleaner than you found them; notify your instructor if the eluent is low.

4.1.5 Data Processing

  • Export your data files as a CSV files.
  • You may choose to integrate your peaks in the HPLC software or Jupyter.
  • You should prepare your calibration curve in Jupyter.
  • You should calculate the concentration of your samples in Jupyter.
  • You should use a t-test to determine if the mass of acetaminophen is significantly different from the expected mass.
