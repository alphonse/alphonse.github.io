3.2 HPLC

The general order for HPLC sample preparation is: (1) Prepare standard and quality control sample (QC), (2) run standards and QC, (3) prepare the samples, (4) run the samples. You should obtain a clean blank, passing standard curve, and passing QC before preparing your samples. If any of these fails there is no point in running your samples!

For HPLC, the definitions of passing are:

  1. Blank: Flat, smooth baseline showing only random noise and/or instrument drift no greater than a few percent over 30 minutes.
  2. Standard Curve: A linear line with \(R^2 \ge 0.9990\).
  3. QC: A measured concentration within 5% (\(\pm\) 5%) of the known concentration.

3.2.2 Instrumentation: Standards

  1. Warm up the HPLC according the the HPLC SOP.
  2. Place your standards in the HPLC autosampler.
  3. Prepare a sequence based on the autosampler locations of your samples (refer to HPLC SOP for more info). Prepare your sequence in this order:
    1. Zero Standard (blank)
    2. Low Standard
    3. Medium-low Standard
    4. Medium Standard
    5. Medium High Standard
    6. High Standard
    7. QC Sample
  4. After the instrument has warmed up (stable baseline), run your sequence.
  5. Analyze your standard curve.

A passing blank will show no signs of contamination.
A passing standard curve will have \(R^2 \ge 0.9990\).
A passing QC will be within \(\pm\) 5% of the known concentration.

Checkpoint:

  • A passing blank will show no signs of contamination.
  • A passing standard curve will have \(R^2 \ge 0.9990\).
  • A passing QC will be within \(\pm\) 5% of the known concentration.

If any of these is not passing you must repeat the analysis and/or remake your standards until they pass!

3.2.3 Sample Preparation

Repeat the following for each sample:

  1. Tare an HPLC sample vial.
  2. Place 4 drops of e-cigarette liquid in the vial.
  3. Fill to top with approximately 2 mL ultrapure water.
  4. Obtain the mass of the water you added.

3.2.4 Instrumentation: Samples

  1. Warm up the HPLC according the the HPLC SOP (skip if already warm).

  2. Place your standards in the HPLC autosampler.

  3. Prepare a sequence based on the autosampler locations of your samples (refer to HPLC SOP for more info). Prepare your sequence in this order:

    1. Standards & QC (see HPLC | Instrumentation: Standards section)
    2. Up to 10 samples
    3. QC Sample (same as previous QC; always finish with QC)

  4. After the instrument has warmed up, run your sequence.

  5. Analyze your standards and samples.

    You should re-run your standards when you run your samples. If you’ve already run them within the past 2 hours you do not need to re-run them.
