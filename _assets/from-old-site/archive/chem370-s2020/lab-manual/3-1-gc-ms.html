3.1 GC-MS

Safety

This lab requires methanol for solvents. Methanol can cause blindness and absorbs through the skin. Wear gloves and goggles when working with methanol, and use it in a hood.

Students should take care when working with the microliter syringe to avoid piercing their skin.

3.1.1 Sample Preparation

  1. Place 5 drops of the e-cigarette liquid in a 2-mL GC autosampler vial.
  2. Clean a microliter syringe with the syringe cleaning apparatus.
  3. Load the syringe with 2 \(\mu\)L of methanol.
  4. Place the syringe in an arm clamp/ring stand and push the GC autosampler vial onto the tip of the syringe. Do NOT let the tip touch the liquid in vial!
  5. Push the plunger of the syringe down all the way so that you have a small drop of methanol on the end of the syringe. Do not let the drop touch anything!
  6. Expose the drop of solvent for 30 seconds and then pull the plunger back up to pull the methanol back into the syringe.
  7. Inject the sample into the GC immediately.

3.1.2 Instrumentation

  1. Run on GC using the CHEM370_Vape.M method and the GC-MS SOP.
